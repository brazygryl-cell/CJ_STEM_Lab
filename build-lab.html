Build Lab

Hands-on STEM challenges for builders, inventors, and problem-solvers.

Build Lab focuses on engineering design, prototypes, invention planning, structures, testing, redesign, and reflection. These activities help students turn ideas into something they can sketch, build, test, explain, and improve.

What students practice

Build Lab helps learners think like engineers.

Students are encouraged to ask questions, imagine solutions, plan their designs, create prototypes, test their work, and make improvements based on evidence.

Plan

Students identify the problem, list constraints, choose materials, sketch ideas, and explain their design choices.

Build

Learners create models or prototypes using available materials, structure, creativity, and problem-solving strategies.

Improve

Students test their designs, collect observations, revise their work, and reflect on what changed and why.

Build Activities

Engineering activities and challenge ideas.

These Build Lab activity categories can be used for classroom projects, enrichment, tutoring, STEM clubs, family STEM nights, or independent practice.

Structures

Bridge & Tower Challenges

Students design structures that balance height, strength, stability, materials, and testing criteria.

Engineering

Moon Lander Engineering

Learners create a lander that protects a payload during impact, then redesign based on test results.

Innovation

STEM Fair Inventions

Students develop original solutions for everyday problems using research, planning, prototyping, and presentation.

Design

Prototype Design

Students move from idea to labeled sketch to simple model while explaining how each feature solves a problem.

Challenge

Problem-Solving Challenges

Open-ended challenges ask students to work within limits, test ideas, and justify their decisions.

Reflection

Engineering Reflection Sheets

Reflection tools help learners explain what worked, what failed, and how they would improve the next version.

Featured Challenge

Build a classroom helper prototype.

Design a simple invention that could make school, studying, organizing, reading, cleaning, carrying supplies, or focusing easier for students.

The goal is not to make the “perfect” product. The goal is to identify a real problem, design a possible solution, explain the features, and improve the idea after feedback.

Try This Challenge

Challenge Steps

  1. Choose a school-related problem.
  2. Sketch a solution with labels.
  3. List at least three useful features.
  4. Build a simple model or prototype.
  5. Test, improve, and explain your redesign.

Flexible materials

Build activities can work with simple supplies.

Build Lab activities are designed to be flexible. Many challenges can be completed with classroom supplies, recycled materials, craft items, or simple household materials.

Simple Supplies

Paper, cardboard, tape, string, straws, cups, craft sticks, foil, or pipe cleaners.

Design Tools

Sketch paper, planning templates, rulers, labels, diagrams, and reflection sheets.

Testing Tools

Weights, timers, measuring tools, drop zones, checklists, and observation charts.

Presentation Tools

Display boards, slides, photos, short videos, written explanations, and rubrics.
